Physical Health Issues

Eye Strain (Computer Vision Syndrome)

Long screen time can lead to blurred vision, headaches, and dry eyes. The 20-20-20 rule—looking at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds every 20 minutes—helps reduce strain. computer Vision Syndrome (CVS) is a condition caused by prolonged use of digital screens, resulting in symptoms like dry eyes, blurred vision, headaches, and neck or shoulder pain. The strain occurs because screens require intense focus, often causing us to blink less, leading to eye dryness and discomfort. To prevent CVS, follow a few simple steps: —reduce screen brightness and glare by adjusting room lighting and using anti-glare filters; and enable blue light filters on your devices, especially in the evening. Frequent blinking also helps keep your eyes moist. If symptoms continue, it's best to consult an eye specialist for tailored advice.

Musculoskeletal Problems

Musculoskeletal problems related to digital device use are common, especially with prolonged sitting or poor posture, which can cause issues like back pain, neck strain, and carpal tunnel syndrome. These issues arise when muscles, joints, and tendons are overused or placed in uncomfortable positions for extended periods.

Repetitive Strain Injuries (RSI)

Repetitive Strain Injuries (RSI) are a group of musculoskeletal disorders caused by repetitive motions, overuse, or prolonged strain on certain body parts, often affecting the wrists, hands, shoulders, and elbows. Common activities that lead to RSI include typing, mouse use, and prolonged gaming or phone use, which can cause inflammation, pain, numbness, and reduced flexibility in the affected areas.

Mental Health Concerns

Anxiety and Stress

Constant connectivity can increase anxiety.Excessive digital device use contributes to anxiety and stress in several ways. Social media fosters comparison, leading to feelings of inadequacy, while constant notifications create “notification anxiety,” disrupting focus and relaxation. Blue light from screens before bed disrupts sleep, which can increase irritability and stress. Information overload,especially from negative news, can further heighten anxiety levels. Limiting screen time, turning off notifications .

Sleep Disorders

Digital device use, particularly before bed, exposes users to blue light that interferes with melatonin production, a hormone essential for sleep. Poor sleep quality not only leads to physical fatigue but also contributes to anxiety and irritability, creating a cycle of stress.

Social Media & Body Image Issues

Excessive social media use can affect self-esteem. Following positive accounts and engaging in real-life activities can help.

Prevention Tips

  • Take regular breaks, e.g., use the Pomodoro Technique.
  • Adjust brightness and contrast settings on your devices.
  • Set up an ergonomic workspace to reduce strain.
  • Limit screen time before bed to improve sleep quality.
  • Move around every 30-60 minutes to prevent musculoskeletal pain.
  • Drinking plenty of water helps to keep your eyes moist and prevent digital eye strain and dry eyes.
